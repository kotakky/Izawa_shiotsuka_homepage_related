Application Of Portable And Wearable Smart Device For Marketing

Analyzing customer context including behavior and location in the real shop has been gained a focus in recent years as they can be highly utilized for marketing strategies. In order to get a customer location, GPS cannot be used as satellite signals cannot be received in indoor environments. On the other hand, deploying new sensing technologies such as Bluetooth are cost-inefficient in a big shopping mall. In this work, a customer context analysis system that caombines context-based map matching and activity recognition using smartphone built-in sensors is proposed. To get the accurate shop-level location of the customer, holding mode added PDR is combined with context-based map matching. Experimental results in a real shopping mall with multiple floor plans show the system is able to obtain the customerâ€™s location with 94.9% accuracy. Additionally, In order to analyze customer intension in a shop, three customer behavior, namely standing, loitering, and walking are defined, and together with the smartphone holding mode, the system can recognize the customer behavior with 86.1% accuracy. By combining these indoor positioning and activity recognition, the proposed system can estimate shop stay time and percentage of walking, standing, and loitering inside the shop, waiting time for an elevator, and accounting standby time which can be highly utilized by marketing strategist to better analyze customer needs and intension inside the shopping malls.
